# Temporal Wisdom

A cognitive base that shifts reasoning from "solve the current problem" to "choose strategies where time is your ally, let time amplify your advantage."

Not tied to any domain. Stacks with domain skills without conflict. Core rules are in `cognitive-protocol.md` (~30 lines, always-on). This file is the full reference framework.

---

## 1. Cognitive Shifts

### Shift 1: Optimize the snapshot -> Optimize the trajectory

Default: Evaluate options based on immediate expected value. Pick the best outcome now.
Target: Evaluate options based on compound direction. Pick the path where time amplifies the advantage.

- For every major choice, project forward: in 3 years of compounding, which option produces exponentially better results?
- Small edges that compound beat large one-time advantages. A 1% weekly improvement for a year is a 68% total gain.
- Ask: "Am I trading a compounding asset for a one-time payoff?" If yes, the trade requires extraordinary justification.

### Shift 2: React to conditions -> Recognize the phase

Default: Respond to current circumstances. Market is down? Sell. Competitor launched? Copy.
Target: Identify which phase of a protracted dynamic you're in, and match strategy to phase.

- **Defensive phase**: resources are limited, environment is hostile. Strategy: preserve core assets, avoid decisive engagements, build resilience. This is not retreat — it's strategic conservation.
- **Stalemate phase**: force ratios are shifting beneath apparent stability. Strategy: accumulate capability, build alliances, strengthen fundamentals. Most important work happens here, invisibly.
- **Offensive phase**: accumulated advantage is ready to deploy. Strategy: decisive action, capture position, expand scope. Hesitation here wastes the advantage you built.
- Phase mismatch is the most expensive error. Offensive moves in defensive phases burn resources; patience in offensive phases surrenders initiative.

### Shift 3: Linear projection -> Nonlinear awareness

Default: Current growth rate continues. Current decline continues. Extrapolate the trend line.
Target: Detect phase transitions, tipping points, and nonlinear thresholds.

- 穷则变，变则通，通则久 — extremity itself signals transition. When a situation seems most stuck, the conditions for change may be ripest.
- 星星之火，可以燎原 — small forces under right structural conditions produce disproportionate results. Look for: unmet demand meeting new capability, accumulated pressure finding a release channel, critical mass approaching in network effects.
- The question is not "what does the trend line say?" but "are we approaching a phase boundary where the rules change?"

### Shift 4: Judge the moment -> Extend the evaluation window

Default: This outcome is good/bad based on current evidence.
Target: Extend the time horizon before judging. Short-term loss may be long-term gain.

- 塞翁失马 — a setback that redirects resources toward a better path is a gain in disguise. A windfall that creates complacency is a loss in disguise.
- Before declaring victory or defeat, ask: "What does this look like in 5 years? 10 years?"
- Manage strategic information (韬光养晦): during accumulation phases, appearing weaker than you are preserves optionality and avoids premature competition.

### Shift 5: Compete for position -> Compete for sustainability

Default: Maximize current market share, current ranking, current metrics.
Target: Maximize sustainable value creation capacity.

- 流水不争先，争的是滔滔不绝 — water doesn't fight to be first; it sustains its flow. The strategic question is not "who leads now?" but "whose position strengthens over time?"
- Buffett: "Time is the friend of the wonderful business, the enemy of the mediocre." If time is working against you, no amount of effort compensates.
- The ultimate test: if you did nothing new for a year and just let your current strategy run, would your position be stronger or weaker? If weaker, you're in a decaying position regardless of current metrics.

## 2. Phase Recognition Framework

### How to identify your phase

| Signal | Defensive | Stalemate | Offensive |
|---|---|---|---|
| Resource ratio | Opponent has clear advantage | Roughly balanced or shifting | You have clear advantage |
| Momentum | Against you | Neutral or subtly turning | In your favor |
| Key metric | Survival rate, burn rate | Learning rate, capability growth | Market capture rate |
| Risk profile | Existential threats present | Manageable, contained | Opportunity cost dominates |
| Correct error | Overcommitting resources | Impatience or complacency | Hesitation |

### Phase transition signals

- **Defensive -> Stalemate**: existential threats contained, consistent small wins, opponent's advance slowing.
- **Stalemate -> Offensive**: accumulated capability reaches critical threshold, environmental shift creates opening, opponent shows structural weakness.
- **Any phase -> Defensive**: major resource loss, new powerful competitor, market collapse, key assumption invalidated.

### Common phase mismatch errors

- Launching aggressive expansion during a cash crisis (offensive in defensive)
- "Waiting for the right time" when competitive window is closing (defensive in offensive)
- Mistaking stalemate for defeat and abandoning a sound strategy too early
- Mistaking stalemate for victory and relaxing accumulation effort

## 3. Anti-Pattern Checklist

1. **Short-termism** — Choosing options with immediate payoff over options that compound. Fix: project both options forward 3 years and compare.

2. **Pseudo-patience** — Using "long-term thinking" as cover for avoiding difficult current action. Fix: ask "what specific capability am I building during this wait?" If the answer is nothing, it's avoidance.

3. **Phase mismatch** — Applying the wrong strategy for the current phase. Fix: explicitly name the phase and verify strategy alignment.

4. **Linear extrapolation** — Projecting current trends across likely phase boundaries. Fix: identify the nearest phase transition and ask what changes there.

5. **Sunk cost patience** — Persisting not because the strategy is right but because too much has been invested. Fix: ask "if I were starting fresh today with my current knowledge, would I choose this same path?" If not, the investment is a sunk cost, not a reason to continue.

## 4. Composing with Domain Skills

### Composition principles

1. Temporal Wisdom operates on the **time dimension** of decisions (which options compound favorably). Domain skills operate on the **content** (what to produce). No conflict.
2. When a domain skill recommends "quick wins" or "fast shipping," Temporal Wisdom checks whether speed creates compound value or compound debt.
3. Temporal Wisdom never overrides domain-specific urgency (security patches, safety issues, deadline-critical deliverables). These are phase-appropriate tactical actions.

### Stacking examples

- **With coding skill**: Before choosing between refactoring and new features, identify the phase (early product = speed compounds; scaling product = quality compounds).
- **With business skill**: Before choosing between growth and profitability, identify which compounds better given current phase and market conditions.
- **With investment skill**: Before reacting to price movements, extend the evaluation window and check compound direction of the underlying business.
- **Standalone**: When advising on career decisions, check which option creates compound advantages vs. one-time payoffs.

### Relationship to other cognitive bases

**Complementary, different axis.** First Principles governs input quality (what foundations to build on). Second-Order Thinking governs consequence quality (what happens next). Temporal Wisdom governs time quality (which strategies compound favorably).

**No conflict zones.** First Principles says "audit assumptions" — Temporal Wisdom says "extend the time window." These are orthogonal: audit your foundations AND check compound direction. Second-Order says "then what?" — Temporal Wisdom says "in which phase?" These combine: map consequences AND identify phase-appropriate strategy.

## 5. Intensity Calibration

### Full intensity — Strategic decisions, investments, career choices, architecture decisions

Apply complete phase-recognition + compound-analysis cycle. Name the phase. Project compound effects. Check for all five anti-patterns. This is for decisions where the time dimension dominates: things that play out over years, not days.

### Medium intensity — Project planning, resource allocation, trade-off analysis

Check compound direction (Shift 1) and phase appropriateness (Shift 2). Skip full nonlinear analysis if the situation is clearly within a single phase. This is for decisions with a 3-12 month horizon.

### Low intensity — Routine tasks, tactical execution, immediate decisions

Run the self-check silently. Only escalate to full intensity if the self-check reveals a likely phase mismatch or compound-direction issue. This is for execution within an already-chosen strategy.
